"What is a dividend?"

A dividend is a distribution of profits by a corporation to its shareholders. When a corporation earns a profit or surplus, it is able to pay a proportion of the profit as a dividend to shareholders.

"What is a shareholder?"

A shareholder is an individual or institution that legally owns one or more shares of stock in a public or private corporation.

My Dividend Investing Strategy

(Disclaimer - I am not a financial advisor, nor am I telling you to invest in these specific stocks.)

Most companies pay a dividend every quarter. They'll have a payout schedule at different months of the year. For example, some companies might pay their dividend on months of January, April, July, and October like JP Morgan. Another company might pay their dividend on February, May, August, and November like Apple.Johnson & Johnson pays their dividend on March, June, September, and December. With these three companies combined, the shareholder is getting a dividend paid out to them every month.

The shareholder can take it a step further by combining several companies that have the same payout for their quarterly months, and finding different payout weeks.

A company is not obligated to pay the shareholder a dividend. They have the right to partially, or fully cut their dividend at any time. With that said, a lot of dividend paying companies have a long history of not only paying their dividend every quarter, but also increasing their dividend over time.

Before you start investing, follow these 3 tips:

  1. Don't invest with emotion.
  2. Invest in companies that you know.
  3. Time in the market beats timing the market.
